In 2009 I learned Dvorak and Vim at the same time.

I was tired of my favorite editors dying on me.  
TextMate, Sublime — gone.  
I figured an investment in Vim would pay off in transferrable productivity.  
And if I was going to be slow anyway, might as well learn a new keyboard layout too.

I made this switch while my newborn was in the hospital with pediatric cancer.

Grasping for control, probably.

When everything that matters is outside your hands — your kid's life, the diagnosis, the next round of tests — you reach for the thing you *can* control.  
For me, that was the tool under my fingers.

I don't think that's noble.  
I think it's just what I did.

And it set a pattern I've repeated for fifteen years:  
When the ground shifts, go deeper.  
Find the layer below.  
Get good enough there that the next earthquake doesn't take you out.

I can be productive in all editors now.  
At least those with good Vim emulation.

That's not nothing.
